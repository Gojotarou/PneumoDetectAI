AI Confidence Score Interpretation

The confidence score represents how clearly the AI model can see and identify pneumonia patterns in the X-ray. A higher score indicates clearer detection of pneumonia features.

0-40%: Low Confidence
The X-ray findings are borderline or minimal. Result may show normal lung or subtle pneumonia features that are difficult to detect. Recommendation: Requires careful clinical review by physician. Consider repeat imaging or patient follow-up for symptom correlation.
40-70%: Moderate Confidence
The X-ray shows moderate signs of the detected condition. Findings are visible but not conclusive. Recommendation: Clinical correlation required. Review patient symptoms, vital signs, and imaging carefully before final diagnosis. Additional tests may be beneficial.
70-85%: High Confidence
The X-ray clearly shows pneumonia features. Strong radiological signs are present. Recommendation: High likelihood of pneumonia diagnosis supported by imaging. Proceed with clinical confirmation and treatment planning.
85-99%: Very High Confidence
The X-ray shows classic pneumonia findings with very clear patterns. Definitive radiological signs are evident. Recommendation: Strong pneumonia diagnosis confirmed by imaging. Immediate clinical assessment and treatment initiation recommended.

Note: AI score is an assistive diagnostic tool. Final clinical diagnosis must be made by qualified medical professionals considering patient history, symptoms, clinical examination, and all available diagnostic information.

CURB-65 Severity Score

CURB-65 is a clinical tool to assess pneumonia severity and mortality risk. Points are awarded for:

  • Confusion (new onset mental status changes)
  • Urea >7 mmol/L (elevated blood urea)
  • Respiratory rate ≥30 breaths/min
  • Blood pressure (SBP <90 or DBP ≤60 mmHg)
  • 65 = Age ≥65 years
Score 0-1: Low Risk
Mortality risk: 1-2%
Recommendation: Outpatient treatment. Can manage safely at home with close follow-up.
Score 2: Intermediate Risk
Mortality risk: 9%
Recommendation: Consider hospitalization or close clinical supervision. May require IV antibiotics and monitoring.
Score 3: High Risk
Mortality risk: 15%
Recommendation: Hospitalization strongly recommended. Requires close monitoring and intensive care consideration.
Score 4: Very High Risk
Mortality risk: 40%
Recommendation: Hospitalization essential. ICU admission should be considered.
Score 5: Severe Risk
Mortality risk: 57%
Recommendation: ICU admission recommended. Requires intensive management and organ support.

Note: This score should be used alongside clinical judgment. Individual patient factors and comorbidities may affect management decisions.
